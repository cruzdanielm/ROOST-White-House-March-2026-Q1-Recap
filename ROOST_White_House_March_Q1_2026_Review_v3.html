Executive Summary

Ramp delivered — March reached stabilization at 88% occupancy and a $71K NOI print.

ROOST White House completed its first full quarter of operation in Q1 2026. March delivered $262K of Room Revenue on 87.8% occupancy, producing $71K of Net Operating Income — the property's first month of meaningful bottom-line contribution. The ramp from October 2025 (21.5% Occ) to March 2026 (87.8% Occ) is the defining feature of the period: quarter-to-date Room Revenue of $525K translated to $31K of YTD NOI as January and February absorbed pre-stabilization fixed-cost load. With MPI of 108.5 for March (101.4 YTD), the asset has closed the ramp on volume; the near-term lever is rate, where ARI sits at 86.4 (March) and 91.4 (YTD). March's +$1,979 (+2.9%) NOI beat to budget is the first favorable stabilized P&L print for the property.

The one-line read

Occupancy outran plan by nearly 28 points in March, ramp is effectively complete, and the property crossed into positive NOI — the quarter's $31K bottom line is entirely a March story. ADR sat roughly $67 below budget per month-end commentary, so the top-line outperformance is volume-led, mirroring the pattern seen across the broader ROOST portfolio. STR indices confirm the market read: MPI 108.5 (winning share on occupancy), ARI 86.4 (rate lag vs the competitive set), RGI 93.8 (net RevPAR approaching parity as ramp closes). The watch items heading into Q2 are (1) converting occupancy wins into ADR recapture in a DC market where peak season arrives in April–June, (2) extending named-account production beyond the current OTA-weighted mix, and (3) protecting the guest-sentiment lead — Revinate rating of 4.67 holds #1 in the comp set, but NPS slipped to 65.1 (-16% QoQ) and housekeeping sentiment cratered, flagging a room-presentation consistency issue to address before the Q2 volume climb.

What defined the quarter

Performance highlights

  • Ramp achieved. Occupancy trajectory Oct '25 21.5% → Nov 42.0% → Dec 54.2% → Jan 68.1% → Feb 76.4% → Mar 87.8%. The property entered stabilization inside Q1.
  • March profitability. NOI of $71K on $262K Room Revenue — a 27.1% NOI margin in the first stabilized month, with Total Compensation held to $56K and Marketing to $13K.
  • Market share won. March MPI 108.5 and YTD MPI 101.4 — the asset is outperforming the DC competitive set on occupancy in its very first stabilized print.
  • Reputation foundation — with a Q4-to-Q1 softening. Revinate Q1 rating 4.67 (comp-index 104.4, rank #1 of 6), Positive Reviews 95%, Recommended 100%. NPS 65.1 (-16% vs Q4's 77.78) and Cleanliness sentiment 34 (vs Q4's 85) flag housekeeping consistency as the Q2 operational priority, detailed in Section 8.

Strategic areas of focus

Three items to manage into Q2

Rate positioning. ARI 86.4 in March and 91.4 YTD — a rate gap to the competitive set that ran ~$67 below budget on ADR per month-end commentary. April MTD RGI 83.9 and the soft weekly print (RGI 62.5 for Apr 12–18) confirm rate is the bigger headwind as ramp volume stabilizes.

Revenue mix diversification. Current Q1 production leans heavily on OTA parents (Expedia Group ~$101K, Booking Holdings ~$78K), with the remainder flowing through retail, loyalty, and corporate distribution channels. The direct-corporate, government, diplomatic, and cultural-sector named-company base — anchored by US Department of State ($16K) and Washington National Opera ($9K) — is the seed book that needs deliberate scaling in Q2.

Reputation velocity & housekeeping consistency. Revinate 4.67 holds #1 in the comp set on rating, but TripAdvisor Popularity Index of 0.19 ranks #6 of 6 (DC rank #139) — volume-build is still the highest-leverage external reputation lever. Internally, the Q1 sentiment read flags a housekeeping issue: Cleanliness sentiment 34 (vs Q4 85), with Room Cleanliness and Bed the two negative themes driving NPS down 12.7 points QoQ.

What it means
